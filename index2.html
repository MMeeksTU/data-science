The Data

This data comes from the Crowd Counting Consortium which analyzes every protest, demonstration, rally, vigil, etc. throughout the U.S. The creators of this data have coded in many aspects of protests including its location, size, claim, groups, interactions among protestrs, interactions among police, and many others. I chose this data because I should give me an abundance of information that will allow me to ask and potentially answer many types of questions. The initial question I hope to investigate with this milestone is how the frequency of protests have changed since 2017. With major elections, COVID, and massive public responses to policies and governmental actions, I expect to find a lot of interesting statistics that will shape the next stages of this project.

Conclusions on the Data and Where to Go From Here

There seems to be a rather intersting pattern of protest frequency throughout the years as most years are rather stable, but then they shoot up quite intensely in 2019 and 2023. I was rather shocked to see the 2020 numbers so low since, even during the lockdown, protests were at the forefront of our media.

I am curious of a few things in this data set. First, I am wondering if there is any significant difference between political affiliation frequency throughout the years. Did certain election cycles or events bring more people of certain ideologies out to protest? Second, with 2023 being one of the hottest summers in recent record, why is 2023 such an outlier for frequency? Lastly, is there a relationship between the upsurge in 2019 and 2023? They are both before voting years, so would that make a difference?

The next stages of this project will be focussed on which of these questions to answer and possibly finding more.
